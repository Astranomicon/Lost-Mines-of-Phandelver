**Ulgoth's Beard** is a small, weathered village perched along the cliffs of the **Sword Coast**, not far north of Baldur’s Gate. Unlike the great cities of the coast, Ulgoth’s Beard is quiet and unassuming at first glance—a place of salt-stained wood, creaking docks, and winds that carry the scent of the sea through every crooked street.

The village owes its existence to trade and passage along the coast, serving as a modest stop for travelers, fishermen, and the occasional merchant ship. Its harbor, though small, provides safe anchorage against the often unpredictable waters of the Sea of Swords. Life here moves at a slower pace, shaped by the tides and the steady routines of those who have lived their whole lives within sight of the waves.

Despite its size, Ulgoth’s Beard has a reputation for drawing in the unusual. Strange travelers pass through with surprising frequency, and rumors of odd happenings are common currency among its locals. Whether it’s whispers of hidden ruins nearby or peculiar visitors who linger just a little too long, the village seems to sit on the edge of something more than it appears. Those who stay for any length of time often discover that beneath its quiet exterior lies a place touched by mystery.

For adventurers, Ulgoth’s Beard is less a destination and more a threshold—a place where journeys pause, rumors take root, and the next path forward begins along the rugged coastline or out across the open sea.
