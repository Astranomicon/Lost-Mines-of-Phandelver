# **Chapter 1: What *Is* a Roper?**
Ropers are:
- Rock-like monstrosities
- Bad at parties
- Excessively grabby
- Fond of dragging adventurers into their terrible tooth-filled maws

They look like stalagmites. They act like nightmares.

# **Chapter 2: Anatomy of the Beast**
Ropers have several key features adventurers should fear—*ahem*—recognize.

## **Tentacles**
- Reach farther than seems physically reasonable
- Sticky
- Strong enough to yoink a barbarian off their feet

## **Mouth**
- Full of teeth
- Bigger than it looks
- Sometimes smiling (this is not good)

## **Hide**
- Rock-like, incredibly tough
- Immune to: common sense, polite negotiation

# **Chapter 3: The Roper's Tactics**
#### **1. Playing Dead (Their Favorite)**
They lie still. They wait. They listen.

They let you walk *right up to them.*

Then they grab you.

> **Scrawled note:** *Do not poke strange cave formations. Learned this the hard way.*

#### **2. The Yoink-and-Chomp**
A Roper grabs you with its tentacles and drags you into its mouth.

**Do not get dragged into the mouth.**

#### **3. Splitting the Party (Literally)**
Tentacles hitting multiple targets create chaos. Ropers love chaos. They thrive in it.

#### **4. Positioning**
They cling to walls, ceilings, floors—whatever gives them the advantage.

---

# **Chapter 4: How to Survive a Roper Encounter**

#### **Tip #1: Don't Split the Party**
They can drag people off. Don’t help them.

#### **Tip #2: Blunt Weapons Are Your Friend**
Their stony hide hates bludgeoning.

> **Margin scribble:** *Mages throwing pebbles counts. Barely.*

#### **Tip #3: Fire Helps**
Ropers dislike extreme temperatures. Fire or cold can slow them.

#### **Tip #4: Avoid Being Tasty**
Heavy armor wearers: You are snacks.

#### **Tip #5: Identify Before You Step**
If you enter a cave and see:
- suspiciously placed lone stalagmites,
- unusual silence,
- your rogue sweating,

you may be near a Roper.

# **Chapter 5: Weaknesses (They Do Have Some!)**
- **Bludgeoning Damage:** Works wonders.
- **Fire:** Makes them shriek.
- **Being Ignored:** They hate when prey stays out of reach.
- **Bright Light:** They prefer dark caves.

#### **The Greatest Weakness of All:**
**Distance.**
If they can’t reach you, they can’t eat you.

> *I recommend crossbows. Or a wizard with good aim.*

# **Chapter 6: Things You Should Absolutely Avoid Doing**
❌ Approaching a lone stalagmite

❌ Turning your back on anything rocky

❌ Throwing your torch to "get a better look"

❌ Trying to reason with it (“We can share the cave!”)

❌ Making loud noises (they like that)

❌ Licking the cave walls (unrelated to ropers, just… don’t)

# **Chapter 8: Field Notes from Real Encounters**
Collected from my journal (the pages not covered in blood, ink, or tears).

#### **Case Study 1 – "The Silent Cavern"**
I entered a cavern so quiet I could hear my own heartbeat. I should’ve turned around immediately. Instead, I took three steps in and noticed a "stalagmite" that somehow hadn’t been there moments before.

> *Footnote:* "I swear it moved. I SWEAR."

#### **Case Study 2 – "The Barbarian Toss"**
Our party barbarian, Kragg, was grabbed, lifted, and *yeeted* across the cavern with such force he left an imprint on the wall. He survived. The wall did not.

#### **Case Study 3 – "The Ceiling Surprise"**
Fun fact: Ropers can hang from ceilings. Less fun fact: This is typically above your squishiest party member.

# **Chapter 9: Signs You Are Already Doomed**
If you experience any of the following, please prepare final remarks:

#### **1. Sudden, inexplicable silence**
If even the rogue stops cracking jokes… something is very wrong.

#### **2. The "Rock" You Passed Twice**
If you see the same stalagmite in two different places… run.

#### **3. Tentacle Shadows That Should Not Exist**
If shadows are moving while your party is not, you have mere seconds.

#### **4. Party Members Being Dragged Away**
If one companion is yoinked, shame on the party.
If two companions are yoinked, shame on the roper.
If three companions are yoinked, shame on *you*—you’re next.

#### **5. Your Torch Goes Out "For No Reason"**
It's not "no reason." It’s the roper.

# **Chapter 10: Roper Myths Debunked**
#### **Myth 1: Ropers sleep like normal creatures.**
**FALSE.** Ropers do not sleep. They *lurk.*

#### **Myth 2: Ropers only live in caves.**
**MOSTLY FALSE.** They prefer caves, but anywhere dim, rocky, and death-filled works.

#### **Myth 3: Ropers can be tamed.**
**DEAR GOD, NO.** Anyone who attempts this becomes a snack.

> *Footnote scribble:* "Looking at you again, Tharnin."

#### **Myth 4: They cannot move.**
**PARTIALLY TRUE.** They *can* move—they just wait for the most dramatic moment possible.

#### **Myth 5: They’re made of rock.**
**FALSE.** They’re made of nightmares and spite.
