A name has begun to surface in whispers and bloodstained walls:

You do not yet know who—or what—this refers to. But wherever the name appears, it is tied to ritualistic death and something far more deliberate than simple murder.

## Lucien’s Investigation

Your ally Lucien has been tracking a pattern beginning in Baldur's Gate and moving north along the Sword Coast.

In every location, the same pattern repeats:

- He arrives **shortly after** something has occurred
- The perpetrators are **already gone**
- What remains is **eerily consistent**

## Description

The **Cult of the Undying King** is not a name spoken loudly in taverns or town squares. It is whispered—if at all—between uneasy glances and half-finished thoughts, as though speaking it too clearly might invite its attention. Across the Sword Coast, a pattern has begun to emerge: a trail of violence that feels ritualistic, deliberate, and deeply wrong.

Those who have come across the aftermath rarely forget it. The scenes are disturbingly consistent. A single victim—stripped bare—laid out upon a table or altar. A precise wound driven into the chest, as though the heart itself were the target of some grim purpose. Blood is not merely spilled; it is used. Smeared across walls, floors, and sometimes even ceilings, forming strange and unsettling symbols that no common villager can identify, yet all instinctively fear. Whatever these markings represent, they are not random—they are messages, prayers, or perhaps offerings to something unseen.

What makes the cult most unnerving is not just the brutality, but the timing. In town after town, those who follow the trail—like Lucien—arrive just a little too late. The perpetrators are always gone. No witnesses. No suspects. Only silence and the lingering sense that something has already been taken—not just life, but something deeper. Something intangible.

And yet, recently, something has changed.

For the first time, there is a survivor.

Corvin should be dead. By all accounts, he _was_ dead—laid out like the others, marked by the same ritual wound. But somehow, impossibly, he lives. Whether this is a failure of the cult’s ritual, an unintended consequence, or something far more significant is unclear. What _is_ clear is that Corvin represents something new: a break in the pattern. A question the cult has not yet answered.

No one truly knows what the Cult of the Undying King seeks. Some believe they worship a being that transcends death itself—an ancient power that promises eternal life at a terrible cost. Others think they are chasing forbidden knowledge, trading blood and secrets in equal measure to uncover truths mortals were never meant to hold. Whatever the case, their actions suggest purpose, patience, and an unsettling degree of coordination.

They are not chaotic killers.

They are following a design.

And now, for the first time, that design has a flaw.
