_“The sea keeps secrets. We keep the dead from joining them.”_

### Public Reputation

To travelers:  
They are fishermen, lighthouse keepers, and tide-wardens.

To locals:  
They are the reason children still sleep at night.

To necromancers:  
They are a recurring problem that refuses to die.

## Founding Legend

About **47 years ago**, Ulgoth’s Beard experienced what the locals call:

### _The Drowned Season_

For thirteen nights the tide did not behave normally.

- The sea withdrew too far
- Nets came back filled with bones
- Crabs crawled inland and died
- Fog never lifted

Then the bells began ringing.

Not the temple bells.

The **shipwreck bells offshore** — long sunken buoy bells — began tolling every night at low tide.

And then the dead came ashore.

Not zombies.

Not skeletons.

**Ghosts. Sailors. Fishermen. Drowned wives. Children lost to storms.**

But they were _angry_.  
Something in the Shadowfell was pulling at them — a summoning beacon placed by a necromancer attempting to weaken the veil between sea and death.

The Flaming Fist never arrived in time.

The town survived because of **one retired sailor-priest named Edrick Marr**, who discovered:

> Saltwater consecrated with funeral rites _burned ghosts._

He organized villagers, forged harpoons into weapons, lit bonfires along the coast, and fought spirits for six nights.

By dawn of the seventh day, only twelve people were left alive.

Those twelve founded the Order.

---

# Purpose of the Order

The Brinebound Lantern does not hunt undead in general.

They hunt **restless dead specifically created or manipulated by planar interference.**

Their true belief:

> Ghosts are not enemies.  
> Ghosts are _symptoms._

They believe a strange cult is weakening the boundary between the Shadowfell and the Material Plane — and the ocean is the thinnest place.

Why the ocean?  
Because the sea already _takes souls without rites._

That makes it the perfect place to create uncontrolled undead.

---

# Headquarters — The Saltfire Lighthouse

A black-stone lighthouse built atop an older ruin.

Important detail:  
The lighthouse predates the town.

The Order discovered its foundation is constructed from **grave-marker stone** and contains a sealed spiral stair descending into a sea cave… where the original Shadowfell anchor was placed.

They guard it constantly.

### Features Inside

- walls carved with funeral prayers in multiple languages
- ropes of dried kelp hanging like prayer beads
- shelves of labeled jars containing: grave sand, bone ash, salt, whale oil
- a bell that rings when incorporeal undead enter 300 ft

The beacon itself is not normal fire.

It burns:  
**Whale oil mixed with cremation ash and consecrated salt.**

Ghosts _cannot pass through its light._

## Structure of the Order

### Lanternbearers (Novices)

Young recruits and survivors of hauntings.

Training:
- burial rites
- identifying death curses
- recognizing possession
- keeping vigil without sleep

They carry:  
salt lanterns and small bells.

---

### Tidewardens (Hunters)

Field operatives and investigators.

They travel the coastlines and inland villages quietly.

They are not famous because they **avoid attention deliberately** — necromancers hunt them first.

Abilities they specialize in:
- exorcisms
- spirit tracking
- anchoring souls to rest sites

---

### The Drowned Vigil (Elite Slayers)

The true ghost-killers.
Every member has **died once**.

No exception.
Each voluntarily undergoes a ritual called:

#### The Quieting

They are drowned in the tide cave for 3 minutes while priests chant their funerary rites.

When revived:

- ghosts cannot possess them
- they see into the Ethereal Plane at all times
- they slowly lose warmth in their body over years

They age very slowly but become emotionally distant.

--- 

## Key Members

### High Warden Maelis Thornwake

Leader of the Order.

Formerly a fisherman's daughter who was possessed as a child and survived.

She is calm, blunt, and frighteningly practical.

Maelis teaches her order:

> “The dead do not gather without a shepherd.  
> Spirits wander, yes… but they do not organize.  
> Something is calling them — not to rise… but to listen.”

She has noticed that certain spirits attempt to _travel inland_, not remain tied to the sea.  
Almost as if they are trying to reach a destination they themselves don’t understand.

She believes the cult is:

- testing locations
- weakening barriers
- searching for a place where “the veil tears cleanly”

And she is convinced Ulgoth’s Beard is only one of many sites.

---

### Brother Caldrin Marr

Grandson of the founder.

Kind, exhausted, and sleep-deprived. He performs most funerals personally so souls don’t linger.

He secretly hears whispers from the sea every night — a buried artifact offshore is still active.

---

### The Pale Diver

A legendary Drowned Vigil slayer.

Never removes his diving cloak or barnacle-covered mask.

Rumor:  
He died 4 times.

Truth:  
He has no heartbeat anymore but is not undead.
He can walk underwater along the seabed and hunt drowned spirits.
