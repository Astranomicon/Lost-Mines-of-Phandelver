A wisp of a cat, Buddy is a small tortoiseshell tom who has clearly seen better days. His fur is ragged, belying his age, and several chunks are missing from his ears. He wheezes slightly with every breath. Despite his obvious frailty, his green eyes burn with intensity and purpose. He is affectionate, weaving easily between the legs of anyone who greets him kindly.

“I am Dark Song—once my colony’s finest warrior, now the guardian of this place. You would do well to treat Big Cat and Big Kitten kindly, for my rages were once the legend of my people. In my old age, I left my colony; retirement did not suit my spirit. Now I stand firm against the forces of evil in this furless colony. Tell me—outsiders, you do not conspire with the Goose, do you?”

**About Buddy**

**Q.** Hello, Buddy! How are you, little kitty?  
**A.** I am well—but that is not my name, furless ones.

**Q.** Oh? What is your name?  
**A.** My name is Dark Song. Once, it was War-Cat Dark Song, but those days are behind me. Big Cat calls me “Buddy.” He cannot help his ignorance.

**Q.** Is Big Cat what you call Bart?  
**A.** I believe so. Big Cat is large and unable to hunt. He shuffles about with his tools and his food magic—completely helpless, really—but he is kind, for a furless. The same goes for the Big Kitten who visits often.

**Q.** So you used to be a warrior, but you retired?  
**A.** That is correct. Among my people, I was legendary. The great Sky-Cats in the stars foretold my birth, and with their guidance I defeated many foes. It is said the mark in my ear means I am destined for greatness.

All things come to an end. As my reflexes dulled, I became a liability as my colony’s champion. I left, for there was no place for a warrior’s heart trapped in a failing body. Big Cat took me in, and now I stand guard—watching for the Great Evil, and protecting him as best I can.
